Origin Story

The TMNT franchise began in November 1983 when artist Kevin Eastman drew a sketch of a turtle wearing a ninja mask and wielding nunchucks. He and fellow artist Peter Laird found the concept hilariously absurd and decided to develop it into a full comic book.

The first issue was published in May 1984 by their company Mirage Studios (named because there was no actual studio, just Laird's living room). They printed 3,000 copies using Eastman's $500 tax return, Laird's $200 savings, and $1,300 borrowed from Eastman's uncle.

The Mutation Event

In the original comic, the Turtles' origin is tied to Marvel's Daredevil. A canister of radioactive isotope labeled "T.C.R.I." falls from a truck, strikes a bowl of baby turtles, and plummets into a New York sewer. The turtles are discovered by Splinter, a rat who was once the pet of ninja master Hamato Yoshi, who had been killed by the villainous Oroku Saki (the Shredder).

Meet the Turtles

The four brothers were named after Renaissance artists by their sensei Splinter, each with distinct personalities and weapons:

Key Characters

Master Splinter

Species: Mutant rat
Role: Sensei and father figure to the Turtles
Background: Former pet of Hamato Yoshi; named for his skill at breaking wooden boards

April O'Neil

Occupation: Reporter (varies by version)
Role: Human ally and friend to the Turtles
Significance: Key bridge between the Turtles and the surface world

The Shredder (Oroku Saki)

Affiliation: Leader of the Foot Clan
Role: Primary antagonist
Design inspiration: A metal cheese grater that Eastman thought would make a fearsome weapon

Notable Villains

  • Krang - Brain-like alien warlord from Dimension X
  • Bebop and Rocksteady - Mutant warthog and rhinoceros henchmen
  • Foot Clan - Ninja organization (parody of Daredevil's "The Hand")

Cultural Impact and Legacy

The TMNT franchise exploded in popularity with these milestones:

  1. Comic book debut (1984) - Initially printed 3,000 copies
  2. Animated series launch (1987) - Made the franchise kid-friendly
  3. Toy line release (1988) - Generated $1.1 billion in sales by 1992
  4. Live-action film (1990) - Became highest-grossing independent film at the time
  5. Ongoing reboots and adaptations through 2025
